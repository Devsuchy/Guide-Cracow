Top three activities to do at Cracow

Cracow Błonia

Błonia - a city park in the form of a huge meadow (formerly a communal pasture) with an area of ​​48 ha and a phenomenon of about 3,500 meters, located in Kraków, in the Zwierzyniec Peninsula, west of the Old Town. Contemporary Błonia visible only initially, the great Błonia, are enclosed in a quadrangle - al. 3rd May, ul. Piastowska, ul. Na Błoniach (along the Rudawa River) and al. Marshal Ferdinand Foch.

The Wawel Royal Castle

The Wawel Royal Castle together with the Wawel Hill is one of the most historically and culturally significant places in Poland. For centuries the seat of Polish kings and a symbol of statehood, in 1930 the Castle also became one of the most important museums in Poland.

Krak (Krakus) Mound

Krak (Krakus) Mound and Liban quarry Today it is an interesting and relatively rarely visited by tourists viewpoint. Around, extensive, sunny meadows with benches and views of the center of Krakow and the Liban quarry - perfect for a family picnic. On the Tuesday after Easter, the holiday of the Sleeves is celebrated here - knights' shows combined with a medieval festival.
